The communities we work with are at the heart of planning, designing and creating the artworks.

Dani Lee Arts facilitates and project manages the commissions. Participants to workshops receive inspiring teaching and choose from an extensive range of colourful and textural materials to create their artworks.

Amman Valley Mural

This mural was designed, crafted and installed by a huge amount of amazing individuals who live in and love the Amman valley. To create the design Dani asked the question “What matters to you here?” Through shared stories and sketched scenes we explored the themes that define life in Cwmaman. A collective mural vision emerged - one that celebrates the history, identity, and the spirit of the valley.

For more information see the articles in News

Mosaic Street Mural

The mural was designed and created by many people in the community of Ammanford. The children from Ysgol Bro Banw made some ceramic pieces - the words, the coal and lots of caterpillars. These were then incorporated into the mosaic which was created on mesh in a nearby hall. The community then got involved in installing the artwork on the street.

Click below for full images

“It was fantastic working with Dani. I really enjoyed every minute of the project. It was truly amazing seeing the project move from simple ideas to the final mosaic. Dani has a real gift for inspiring people to create something spectacular.”

“It was a total joy to work alongside you all, a wonderful privilege and something I will cherish for a very long time. I’m so excited still, the mural is just stunning!”

  • Where – Quay Street, Ammanford: Map
  • Partner - The Ammanford Lions Club
  • Funded and Supported by - National Lottery Community Fund, The Old Cross Inn, Carmarthenshire County Council, Ardex

Community Cob Mural

Mural created with the service users of Hafal, a charity that works with those that are experiencing mental health issues. This mural was created from cob, a fun and sticky mixture of sub-soil, sand, straw and water. In suitable locations this mural material lasts surprisingly well.

Click below for full images

“Dani was fun, very patient and involved us in all the processes such as designing the mural, squishing the subsoil clay with water in a big bucket wearing wellies, doing "The Twist" in wellies on a tarpaulin to mix the clay and straw, and creating the design on the wall with the cob mixes. It was great fun, and whether she knows it or not, in doing that cob mural with us, Dani gave us each a gift of some self-esteem, as well as some creative muddy good fun.”

  • Location - The Pensioners Hall, Ammanford
  • Partner - Hafal Ty Aman Centre for Adults Recovering from Severe Mental Illness
  • Funded by - Education 2000
